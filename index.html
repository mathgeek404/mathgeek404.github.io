About Me

After completing high school in 2013, I matriculated into the University of Wisconsin Madison. My primary research interests lie at the intersection of mathematics and computer science, particularly within the fields of data science, machine learning, security, and cryptography. Recently, my career interests have extended into research and innovation regarding virtual reality, video games, and cognitive research.

As a technologist and engineer, I strive to understand the latest hardware through hands-on hacking and testing, from portable ECG devices to new wearable accessories. Most of my days are spent tinkering on personal tech projects, competing at hackathons, and attending tech meetups and workshops. As a traveler and all-around adventurer, I can always be found exploring and enjoying the local activities, culture, and cuisine. When the weather and geography permit, I can be found snowboarding, swimming, biking, surfing, kayaking, hiking, or trying something else entirely. As a self-proclaimed foodie, I will always seek out the most original, off-beat, iconic, top rated, and delicious spots to eat, no matter the distance, the cost, or the challenge. I share my deep rooted passion for gaming, animation, and sci-fi culture through my extracurricular clubs, meetups, and hobbies; I aspire to engineer technologies to augment fans and creators alike, providing venues for deeper connections and more visceral experiences through the arts and entertainment.

Currently, I am a rising senior undergraduate student at the UW Madison, pursuing a B.S. Double Major in Computer Science and Mathematics. Projects

SpeechMaster

Building your linguistic and speaking skills as a non-native speaker can be very difficult. Meanwhile, the fear of public speaking ranks as the number one phobia in the US, affecting 75% of the population. SpeechMaster aims to solve this problem in the most effective and direct manner possible: practice. Utilizing text analytics across several cognitive computing systems, SpeechMaster provides an extensive toolkit focused on quantifying and targetting practice at areas in need of attention based upon sample speeches.

Repetition is key, so SpeechMaster allows users to track their progress in time, utilizes engaging exercises and reccomended resources in order to build their own skills. However, a software application can only provide so much engagement and information; the application integrates with social media to allow users to share their speeches and progress with friends, family, and teachers, whom may provide further feedback, encouragement, and advice.
