About Me

Currently, I am a senior undergraduate student at UW Madison, pursuing a B.S. Double Major in Computer Science and Mathematics. Prior to attending UW, I attended the Massachusetts Academy of Mathematics and Science, enrolling in courses at Worcester Polytechnic Institute as a part of my senior year coursework. After completing high school in 2013, I matriculated into the University of Wisconsin Madison.

My primary career interests lie at the intersection of mathematics and computer science, particularly within the fields of statistics, optimization, data engineering, and security. Recently, my career interests have extended into research and innovation regarding analytical methods in fraud detection, virtual reality, and machine learning.

As a technologist and engineer, I strive to understand the latest hardware through hands-on hacking and testing, from portable ECG devices to new wearable accessories. Most of my days are spent tinkering on personal tech projects, competing at hackathons, and attending tech meetups and workshops.

As a traveler and all-around adventurer, I can’t help but enjoy the activities, culture, and climate a new place has to offer. When the weather and geography permit, I can be found snowboarding, swimming, biking, surfing, kayaking, hiking, or trying something else entirely. As a self-proclaimed foodie, I seek out the most original, off-beat, iconic, top rated, and delicious spots to eat, no matter the distance, cost, or challenge.
